Goodbye Glasgow

3 Years of Southside

Reflecting on the last 3 years

Just over 3 years ago, Katy bought a flat, I escaped Canadian Flavor Lockdown, and then we endured lockdown thinking of the potential of our first flat together. We eventually got in before almost immediately abandoning ship again til the next summer. In a way although it has felt homely, it has never really felt like home. Maybe because of that to-ing and fro-ing it has always felt disjointed here. It is a chapter with multiple threads that never seemed to come together until now as it comes to an end.

Of course there are plenty things that will keep us coming back to Glasgow, so it's less of a goodbye and more of a see you later.

In the Neighbourhood

X Years of Stirling

I'm examining a crystal ball and trying to see what Stirling might have in store for us. Who knows how long we'll be there? Katy of course has been on top of the house hunt for so long that school quality has been a factor in the decision making process. Serendipitously, when we were walking around Stirling after viewing the property we noticed a few coding businesses... And of course, Bannockburn being just down the road gives a lot of historical significance to the area. As already noted, Stirling is central to almost everywhere and we can commute in so many directions.

The flat has room to grow as much as we do. The sun room is surrounded by paving as opposed to garden... and the shed at the back of the garden may see an upgrade so Katy has somewhere to hide all my guitars and noise too!
